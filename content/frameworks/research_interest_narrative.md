# 研究兴趣叙事框架（含兴趣转变）

> **训练原则：** 研究兴趣动机题与兴趣转变题几乎必然连续出现，形成"两连问"。
> 两题的核心策略相同，叙事必须在第一题中就埋下伏笔，使第二题的回答成为自然延伸。
> 永远作为整体练习，不要单独打磨某一题。

---

## 核心策略：延伸，而非转变

**把所有"转变"重新定义为"延伸"。**

绝不承认自己"换方向"了。框架是：底层研究问题始终没变，只是语境变得更当代、更具政策相关性。

> "I wouldn't frame it as a change so much as a natural extension."

---

## 题一：研究兴趣动机

### 一句话核心

从自己过去研究中发现的缺口出发，而不是从外部触发点出发。

### 三层结构

**第一层 — 起点**
从过去研究中发现的某个具体的、未解决的问题或缺口出发。
不能以"我对X感兴趣，因为……"开头。

**第二层 — 延伸**
解释新兴趣如何是对旧问题的自然延伸，而非跳跃。
核心论证：底层研究问题没有变，只是语境或工具变了。

**第三层 — 落地**
给出一个具体的、能用实验经济学语言表述的研究问题。
这个问题必须可以被实验设计检验。

### 核心逻辑

> 不是"我对X感兴趣"，而是"我在研究A时发现了B这个缺口，而X恰好是填补这个缺口的新工具/新视角"。

### 示例回答（英文，直接用于面试）

> "My interest grew out of a gap I noticed in my own research. When I was studying how education pressure affects fertility decisions, I kept finding that the mechanism ran through how families perceive merit and effort—whether success comes from hard work or circumstance. That question felt unresolved.
>
> Then I started thinking: AI tools are now entering classrooms at scale, and they fundamentally blur the line between a student's own effort and machine assistance. That changes how students, teachers, and parents attribute achievement. If early education shapes fairness preferences—as Cappelen et al. show—then AI entering that environment could be a meaningful intervention in how children form those preferences.
>
> So my interest isn't really 'AI' in the abstract. It's about whether AI changes the signals people use to judge merit versus luck—which connects directly to the social preferences research agenda."

### 常见追问

- "Can you give me one concrete research question you would want to answer?"
- "How would you design an experiment to test that mechanism?"
- "What specific mechanism links X to Y?"
- "What papers have you read in this area?"
- "Where do you see yourself contributing to this field?"

## 题二：研究兴趣转变（两连问第二题）

### 三步结构

**第一步 — 找出贯穿所有经历的核心问题**
问自己：所有项目经历中，有没有一条共同的 underlying theme？
用一句话表达这个核心问题。

**第二步 — 说明新方向是同一问题的新维度**
新方向是更有力/更当代/更具政策相关性的版本，而不是替代。

**第三步 — 明确说出"这不是转变，而是延伸"**
> "I wouldn't frame it as a change so much as a natural extension."

### 示例回答（英文，直接用于面试）

> "I wouldn't frame it as a change so much as a natural extension. The thread running through all my previous work is really one question: how do external pressures and institutional environments shape individual behavior and preferences in educational settings.
>
> In my thesis, I was looking at how exam competition changes parents' fertility decisions. In my RA work, I was tracking how school access affects enrollment. The underlying question was always: what forces shape how people make decisions about education and effort?
>
> AI entering the classroom is a new and very powerful version of that same force. It changes the information environment. It changes how effort is attributed. So for me, the research question didn't change—the context became more contemporary and more policy-relevant."

### 常见追问（题二专属）

- "But your methodology background is entirely observational. How will you adapt to experimental work?"
- "What specifically triggered this shift?"
- "Have you done any reading in the new area?"
